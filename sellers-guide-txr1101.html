Seller's Guide to TXR-1101

Residential Listing Agreement — Exclusive Right to Sell — revised 06-15-26

This guide is provided to help explain this agreement in plain language. It is not legal advice and does not replace reading the actual agreement. Paragraph references reflect TXR-1101, revised 06-15-26. Use it with your seller before they sign — not a substitute for reading the actual agreement together.
Paragraph 5 — Fully Negotiable, Nothing Here Is Set by Law or MLS
How Your Broker Gets Paid
¶ 5A

Your broker's fee is whatever you and your broker agreed to — not a standard rate.

¶ 5B–C

The fee is "earned" when you accept any acceptable offer, and "payable" at closing — understand the difference.

¶ 5E

Protection Period: if you sell to a buyer your broker introduced, shortly after the listing ends, the fee may still be owed.

Paragraph 6 — This Decision Affects How Many Buyers See Your Home
Public Marketing & MLS
¶ 6A

If you choose not to file with MLS, your home won't appear on most buyer-facing search sites.

¶ 6B

Photos and descriptions of your home may be used and distributed by the MLS once listed — that's normal, but know it's happening.

Paragraphs 12–13 — Honesty Here Protects You Later
What You're Promising as Seller
¶ 12E–F

Disclose any past-due payments or liens on the home now — surprises later can delay or kill a sale.

¶ 12K

If your home is in a water/utility/improvement district, that needs to be disclosed here.

¶ 13C

Send any buyer who contacts you directly back to your agent — don't negotiate on your own.

Final Checks — Worth Five Minutes
Before You Sign
¶ 19

Confirm which disclosures apply to your home (lead paint if pre-1978, HOA documents, survey, etc.).

Sig. block

This is a legally binding agreement — you're encouraged to have an attorney review it if anything is unclear.

Paragraph 1 — Your Agent Earns the Fee No Matter Who Finds the Buyer
What "Exclusive Right to Sell" Means
¶ 1

You're appointing one broker as your sole agent — even if you find the buyer yourself, the fee is still owed.

¶ 4A

The listing has a clear start and end date — know when it expires.

Paragraph 2 — Worth a Careful Read Before You List
What's Included in the Sale
¶ 2B–C

Built-in items (ceiling fans, fixtures, garage door openers) are presumed included — movable accessories are listed separately.

¶ 2D

Anything you want to take with you must be listed as an Exclusion now, in writing — not negotiated later.

Paragraph 7 — Convenience vs. a Small Added Risk
Showings & Keybox
¶ 7A

Your broker and other agents can access the home at reasonable times to show, inspect, or repair.

¶ 7C

A keybox makes showings easier but does carry some risk (unauthorized entry, theft) — you decide whether to allow one.

¶ 7D

Neither your broker nor other agents are responsible for injury or loss during an authorized showing, except for their own negligence.

Paragraph 9 — Know This Can Happen Before It Does
If Your Agent Represents the Buyer Too
¶ 9

If a buyer your own broker represents wants your home, your broker may act as an intermediary — a neutral role between you and that buyer.

¶ 9 notice

As intermediary, your broker can't reveal you'd accept less, or that a buyer would pay more, without your separate written permission.
