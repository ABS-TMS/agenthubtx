How Rollback Taxes Work in Texas

When land with an agricultural or timber special appraisal (productivity value) changes to a non-agricultural use, the property owner who changed the use owes a rollback tax — the difference between what was paid at the lower agricultural value and what would have been paid at full market value.

Lookback periodThe previous 3 tax years the land received the agricultural appraisalTax Code §23.55
InterestWidely reported at 5% per year since HB 1743 (down from 7%) — confirm the exact current rate with the county
Who owes itThe owner who changes the use — this can be a new buyer if the change happens after a sale
Interest applies toLand qualifying under Subchapter C (1-d) incurs interest; confirm treatment for 1-d-1 (open-space) with the appraisal district

Common Triggers

  • Subdividing or developing the land
  • Building a residential subdivision or paving a commercial site
  • Simply stopping the qualifying agricultural or timber use

A few situations are excepted from the rollback tax — including certain right-of-way sales, condemnations, transfers to a state or political subdivision for public use, and land transferred for economic development purposes.
