Seller's Checklist — TREC No. 20-19

One to Four Family Residential Contract (Resale) — mandatory July 1, 2026

This checklist is a practice aid and does not replace reading the contract or consulting your broker on a specific transaction. Paragraph references reflect TREC No. 20-19. Walk this with the seller at the right moments — not all at once. Each item ties to the paragraph that actually governs it, so you can flip straight to the source.
Paragraphs 1, 2, 3, 4 — Confirm the Deal Actually Works
Before You Accept an Offer
¶ 1–2

Confirm buyer name(s) and entity status match what will go on the deed at closing.

¶ 3

Confirm financing math nets the price you actually agreed to — cash (3A) + financing (3B) = total (3C).

¶ 4

If buyer is financing, confirm pre-approval is current and from a lender you recognize.

Paragraph 5 — Know Your Exposure During This Window
Earnest Money & Option Period
¶ 5A

Confirm earnest money and option fee actually landed with the escrow agent within 3 days of the Effective Date.

¶ 5B

Mark the Option Period end date — buyer can walk for any reason until this deadline passes.

¶ 5A(2)

If a deadline lands on a Legal Holiday (incl. Juneteenth, day after Thanksgiving), confirm the next business day applies.

Paragraph 18 — You Can Keep Showing Unless Told Otherwise
Showings & Marketing During Option Period
¶ 18

Unless expressly prohibited in writing, you may continue showing and accepting back-up offers.

¶ 18

Confirm all representations stay accurate through closing — don't let anything go stale (repairs, condition, occupancy).

Paragraphs 8, 11, 12 — Know What Actually Lands in Your Pocket
Brokers' Fees & Net Proceeds
¶ 8

Confirm broker compensation is reflected correctly — % or $, per the 2026 form update.

¶ 11

Review settlement and other expenses — confirm what's coming out of proceeds at closing.

¶ 12 / 12B

Review the reorganized compensation paragraph and 12B before signing.

Paragraph 7B — This Deadline Has Real Teeth
Seller's Disclosure Timing
¶ 7B

Disclosure Notice delivered on or before the date stated — late delivery can hand the buyer a termination right.

¶ 7B

Confirm every material fact is disclosed — known defects don't get better with time if surfaced later.

¶ 7(I)

Review the groundwater/surface water rights notice before it goes to the buyer.

Paragraph 6 — Clear What You Can Before Objections Land
Title Curative Work
¶ 6A

Order the title commitment early — don't wait on the buyer's side to request it.

¶ 6D

Anticipate objection items (liens, easements, old judgments) and start curing them before the deadline hits.

¶ 6C

Confirm whether you're providing an existing survey or a new one is required, and who's paying.

Paragraph 7 (continued) — Most Negotiation Happens Here
Repairs & Inspection Response
¶ 7

Be ready to respond quickly once the buyer's inspection report comes back — delay can cost the deal.

¶ 7

If lender-required repairs surface post-inspection, confirm in writing who's paying before work starts.

Paragraphs 9, 19, 20, 22 — What You Actually Owe at the Table
Closing & Possession
¶ 9

Confirm possession terms — deliver at closing, or negotiate a temporary lease-back with a per-diem if staying longer.

¶ 19–20

Confirm Federal Requirements (FIRPTA/foreign person status) and FinCEN reporting info is ready for the title company.

¶ 22

Confirm closing date, walk-through timing, and that keys/garage remotes/warranties are ready to hand over.
