Buyer's Checklist — TREC No. 20-19

One to Four Family Residential Contract (Resale) — mandatory July 1, 2026

This checklist is a practice aid and does not replace reading the contract or consulting your broker on a specific transaction. Paragraph references reflect TREC No. 20-19. Walk this with the buyer at the right moments — not all at once. Each item ties to the paragraph that actually governs it, so you can flip straight to the source.
Paragraphs 1, 2, 3, 4 — Parties, Property, Price, Financing
Before You Write the Offer
¶ 1–2

Confirm legal names match ID exactly; confirm the property address, lot/block/subdivision.

¶ 3

Confirm sales price math: cash portion (3A) + financing (3B) = total sales price (3C).

¶ 4

Confirm financing type checked correctly (conventional, FHA, VA, owner financing) and pre-approval is current.

Paragraph 6 — Title Commitment, Survey, Objections
Title & Survey
¶ 6A

Title commitment and exception documents requested — track the delivery deadline (auto-extends if late, per the contract).

¶ 6C

Survey: confirm whether existing survey (T-47/T-47.1) or new survey, and who pays.

¶ 6D

Calendar the objection deadline — buyer must object in writing within the stated window.

¶ 6E(11)

If applicable, confirm seller has disclosed any mold remediation certificate issued in the last 5 years.

Paragraph 7 — Includes New Water Rights Notice (7I)
Property Condition & Disclosures
¶ 7B

Confirm Seller's Disclosure Notice was received — note the date; late delivery can trigger a termination right.

¶ 7(I)

Review the groundwater/surface water rights notice with the buyer.

¶ 7

Walk through utility, propane, and improvement district notices that apply to this specific property.

Paragraph 7 (continued) — Negotiated Outside the Option Period Too
Inspections & Repairs
¶ 7

Schedule the inspection promptly — most repair negotiation happens inside the option period.

¶ 7

If lender requires repairs post-inspection, confirm who is responsible and re-paper if needed.

Paragraph 5 — Most Time-Sensitive Paragraph in the Contract
Earnest Money & Option Period
¶ 5A

Earnest money and option fee delivered within 3 days of the Effective Date — confirm Effective Date is filled in.

¶ 5B

Option Period deadline calendared — this is the buyer's unrestricted right to terminate window.

¶ 5A(2)

If a deadline lands on a Legal Holiday (incl. Juneteenth, day after Thanksgiving), confirm the next business day applies.

Paragraphs 8, 11, 12 — Compensation, Expenses, Prorations
Brokers' Fees & Closing Costs
¶ 8

Confirm how the buyer's broker fee is being paid — reflected as % or $, per the 2026 form update.

¶ 11

Review settlement and other expenses — confirm who pays what at closing.

¶ 12 / 12B

Review the reorganized compensation paragraph and 12B with the buyer before signing.

Paragraphs 9, 18, 19, 20, 22 — The Final Stretch
Closing & Possession
¶ 9

Confirm possession terms — at closing, or a temporary lease-back, and any per-diem if delayed.

¶ 18

Remind buyer all representations survive closing — seller stays liable if something is later untrue.

¶ 19–20

Confirm Federal Requirements (FIRPTA/foreign person status) and FinCEN reporting info is gathered.

¶ 22

Confirm closing date and location with the title company; resolve any last addenda.

Paragraph 21 — Don't Let a Deal Die Over a Missed Notice
Notices & Communication
¶ 21

Confirm correct contact info (buyer, seller, and agents) is filled in — notices are effective once sent to the party or their agent.

¶ 21

Use a delivery method that creates a record (email, overnight courier) for any time-sensitive notice.
