Agent's Checklist — TXR-1101

Residential Listing Agreement — Exclusive Right to Sell — revised 06-15-26

This checklist is a practice aid and does not replace reading the agreement or consulting your broker on a specific listing. Paragraph references reflect TXR-1101, revised 06-15-26. Run this before the seller signs. Every blank, box, and decision point that needs to be right on this form — not a summary of what the agreement means.
Paragraphs 3–4 — No More Standalone Termination-Option Paragraph on This Form
Price & Term
¶ 3

Listing Price filled in as a number — confirm seller signed off on this figure, not a placeholder.

¶ 4A

Listing start date AND end date (11:59 p.m.) both filled in — an open-ended listing is a liability problem.

¶ 4B

If seller already has a pending contract, confirm this listing correctly voids rather than overlaps.

Paragraph 5 — Fully Negotiable, the Form Says So Explicitly Now
Broker Compensation
¶ 5A(1)

Fee filled in as % or flat $ — never leave blank, never imply it's fixed by MLS/Association.

¶ 5A(2)

If charging more when buyer is unrepresented, that % or $ is filled in separately.

¶ 5A note

Seller's authorization to disclose buyer-expense contributions is checked one way or the other, not left blank.

¶ 5E

Protection Period day count filled in — a blank here is unenforceable, not just incomplete.

¶ 5F

County for payment venue filled in.

Paragraph 6 — One Box Only, Don't Leave Both Checked or Both Blank
Listing Services & MLS
¶ 6A

Confirm exactly one of (1) or (2) is checked — file with MLS, or don't.

¶ 6A(1)(b)

If delaying MLS filing, the day count and stated purpose are both filled in, not left blank.

¶ 6B

If filing with MLS, Listing Content license language was reviewed with seller (their content vs. broker's content ownership).

Paragraph 19, Signature Block — The Checklist Before the Checklist Closes
Addenda & Signatures
¶ 19

Every applicable addendum box is checked — especially B (Seller's Disclosure) and C (lead-based paint, if pre-1978).

Sig. block

Broker/Associate name, license number, and signature line all complete — confirm associate authority if signing on Broker's behalf.

All pages

Initialed for Identification line present on every page, not just the signature page.

Paragraphs 1–2 — Get the Legal Description Right, This Is What Gets Conveyed
Parties & Property
¶ 1

Seller name(s) match title exactly; all contact fields filled (phone, email).

¶ 2A

Lot, Block, Addition, County, and address all match the actual legal description.

¶ 2D

Exclusions listed explicitly — anything seller is taking with them must be named here.

¶ 2E

HOA box checked correctly — confirm with the seller, don't assume.

Paragraph 7 — Get This in Writing Before the Sign Goes in the Yard
Access & Keybox
¶ 7B

Scheduling company named, if one is used.

¶ 7C(1)

Keybox authorization checked — confirm seller actually agreed, don't default to yes.

¶ 7C(2)

If tenant-occupied, signed tenant keybox authorization (TXR 1411) collected before placing a keybox.

Paragraph 9 — Moved From Its Old Spot, Don't Skip It Out of Habit
Intermediary
¶ 9

Confirm exactly one of A (Intermediary Status) or B (No Intermediary) is checked, not both, not neither.

¶ 9A

If A is checked, confirm seller understands they still owe the full fee per ¶ 5A even in an intermediary scenario.

Paragraphs 12–13 — New ¶ 12K, Don't Miss the Water District Disclosure
Seller's Representations & Promises
¶ 12E–F

Any delinquent obligations or liens are listed by exception, not silently left blank.

¶ 12I

Relocation company/employer benefit disclosure filled in or explicitly marked none.

¶ 12K

PID/MUD/water district disclosure — confirm seller actually knows the answer, don't guess.

¶ 13C

Seller understands they must refer direct inquiries to Broker, not negotiate independently.
