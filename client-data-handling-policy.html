Purpose & Scope

This policy governs how every agent sponsored by Attorney Broker Services, LLC ("ABS") collects, stores, shares, and disposes of client data. "Client data" means any information you obtain in the course of representing a buyer or seller — including names, contact details, financial information, signed contracts, identification documents, and anything shared with you in confidence during a transaction.

Every agent is responsible for following this policy on every transaction, regardless of deal size or client relationship.

Core Principles
  • Collect only what the transaction requires. Don't ask for or retain financial details, identification, or personal information beyond what's needed for the deal at hand.
  • Store it where it belongs, not wherever is easiest. Transaction documents and client information live in brokerage-approved systems — not scattered across personal email, text threads, or unsecured personal devices.
  • Share on a need-to-know basis. Title companies, lenders, and other parties to the transaction get what they need to do their job — nothing more, and never as a blanket forward of an entire file.
  • Verify before you transmit anything sensitive. Wire instructions, financial documents, and identification get confirmed through a second channel — never acted on from an email alone.
  • Retain only as long as required, then dispose of it properly. Old files sitting indefinitely on a personal device or inbox are a liability, not an asset.
Storage & Retention

Transaction files, signed contracts, and client communications should be kept in the brokerage's designated systems for the duration required by TREC record-keeping rules and brokerage policy. Do not rely on your personal phone or personal Gmail as the only copy of anything — if that device is lost, stolen, or compromised, the data goes with it, and Texas law requires notifying every affected client if that happens.

When a file is no longer needed beyond its required retention period, delete or shred it rather than letting it accumulate indefinitely.

Sharing & Third Parties

It's normal and necessary to share client information with title companies, lenders, inspectors, and other parties to a transaction. Before sharing anything financial or sensitive:

  • Confirm you're sending it to the correct, verified recipient — not just whoever an email thread says they are.
  • Use secure sharing methods (e.g. access-controlled document links) rather than open email attachments where possible.
  • Never act on wire instructions received only by email. Verify by phone, using a number looked up independently, every time — see the Email Security Checklist for the exact script to use.
If You Suspect a Breach
Call your broker immediately — don't wait to confirm it's serious first.

If a device with client data is lost or stolen, if you suspect your email has been compromised, or if you're unsure whether something you sent was intercepted — call Mike directly. Texas law has strict notification requirements once client data may have been exposed, and the timeline starts the moment you know, not the moment it's confirmed.
