Your Obligations to the Buyer Under the TREC 20-19 Contract

The Buyer's Obligations

  • Come from the contract itself
  • Deadlines: earnest money, option fee, inspections, termination notices
  • If missed: buyer loses rights, money, or remedies
  • Tracked on the Buyer Obligations Checklist

Your Obligations

  • Come from statute and your representation agreement
  • Fiduciary duties: care, obedience, loyalty, disclosure, confidentiality, accounting
  • If missed: license discipline, E&O exposure, breach of fiduciary duty claims
  • Mapped to every deadline in this module
Where Your Duties Come From

Four legal sources establish what you owe a buyer client, independent of anything written in the TREC 20-19 contract.

TRELA §1101.557

A broker who represents a party owes that party the duties of a fiduciary.

TRELA §1101.562–.563

Buyer representation must be in writing once you act for the buyer beyond a casual, unrepresented showing.

TRELA §1101.559–.561

Intermediary status — if your brokerage represents both parties — changes which duties apply and to whom.

TREC Fiduciary Duties

Care, obedience, loyalty, disclosure, confidentiality, and accounting are owed throughout representation.

DAY 0 — EFFECTIVE DATE At Signing
§1101.557 — Care

Confirm the effective date is correctly entered (date of final acceptance) before circulating the contract.

§1101.558 — Disclosure

Disclose, in writing, any party or family/business interest you or your brokerage have in the transaction.

Care / Loyalty

Verify the correct financing addendum is attached and matches the buyer's actual loan type.

§1101.562–.563

Confirm a written buyer representation agreement is signed before, or concurrent with, the contract.

WITHIN 3 DAYS — CRITICAL Earnest Money & Option Fee
This is the single most common buyer-side compliance failure. Reminding the buyer is not enough — confirm delivery in writing.
If missed: seller can terminate (5A/5C)

Personally confirm earnest money was delivered to the escrow agent — don't assume.

If missed: buyer forfeits right to terminate (5A/5D)

Personally confirm the option fee was delivered — don't assume.

Care / Accounting

Document delivery confirmation (receipt, email, or escrow confirmation) in the file.

OPTION / INSPECTION PERIOD Coordinating the Option Period
Care

Help schedule inspections with TREC-licensed inspectors and attend or coordinate access.

Care / Disclosure

Confirm any hydrostatic testing is authorized separately in writing before it occurs.

Care

Track the repair amendment or As-Is election deadline (Para 7D) on your own calendar — not just the buyer's.

If the buyer is terminating: personally verify written notice reaches the seller by 5:00 PM on the last day — hand-delivery or confirmed receipt, not just "sent."
Disclosures · Title · Survey

Routing and calendaring review windows the moment documents arrive — not when the buyer says they reviewed them.

Disclosure Duties

  • Forward Seller's Disclosure Notice the day it's received — don't sit on it
  • Same for the Water Disclosure and any Natural Resource Lease notice
  • Calendar each 7-day termination window the moment the disclosure is received
  • Advise on the deadline; the decision to terminate belongs to the buyer (Obedience)

Title & Survey Duties

  • Review the title commitment and exception documents the day they arrive
  • Calendar the written objection deadline (Para 6D) from receipt of commitment, exceptions, and survey
  • Track the seller's 15-day cure period and the buyer's 5-day election window
  • Re-review and re-object to any revised commitment within the same timeframe
FINANCING OBLIGATIONS Watching the Lender Process
Third Party Financing Addendum

Confirm the buyer submitted a loan application promptly and is diligently pursuing financing.

Para 7E — Disclosure

Flag lender-required repairs to all parties immediately — the contract terminates if no agreement is reached.

Para 7E — Care

Calculate whether lender-required repair costs exceed 5% of the sales price — this triggers a termination right with earnest money refund.

Para 12A(2)

Walk the buyer through every line of buyer's expenses due at closing before they're surprised at the table.

Compensation Transparency Is a Duty
Your buyer representation agreement sets your compensation — it is fully negotiable and never set by law, MLS, or association policy.
Disclosure

Disclose in writing how you are compensated before the buyer signs anything.

Para 12B(1)

If a seller offers compensation to the buyer's broker, explain that it is applied toward — and does not change — the buyer's obligation under your agreement.

Loyalty

Never let compensation source influence which properties you show or how hard you negotiate.

Getting to the Closing Table Clean

Before Closing

  • Coordinate the final walk-through and attend if possible
  • Confirm proof of completion for every agreed repair before the buyer signs off
  • Remind the buyer to bind homeowner's insurance ahead of transfer
  • Confirm the buyer has not taken early possession without a TREC lease

At Closing

  • Review closing documents with the buyer before they sign
  • Confirm the earnest money release/application matches Para 18B
  • Flag FIRPTA withholding obligations if the seller is a foreign person with no affidavit
  • Confirm your compensation paperwork (CDA) is accurate before funds disburse
Your Duty Doesn't End at the Table
Para 18C/18D

If the contract terminates, help the buyer respond to an earnest money release request within 7 days — silence creates liability.

Para 16

If a dispute arises, guide the buyer toward mediation before litigation, per the contract.

Para 19 / Accounting

Retain your file — representations and warranties survive closing, and so does your record-keeping duty.

The One-Line Test
Don't Remind. Confirm.

Every duty in this module reduces to the same habit: at every deadline on the buyer's checklist, your job is not to remind the buyer it's due — it's to confirm it actually happened, document that confirmation, and escalate immediately if it didn't.

  • Calendar every deadline the day the contract is signed — not the day before it's due.
  • Confirm delivery in writing for earnest money, option fee, and termination notices.
  • Route every disclosure the day you receive it.
  • Document your advice and the buyer's decisions — especially terminations.
