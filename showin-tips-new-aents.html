Showing Tips for New Agents

What actually trips people up on their first few showings — and how to avoid it

Before the Showing
✅ Confirm the showing agreement is signed before you unlock a door — the moment a showing gets scheduled, not after.
Open Showing Tour →
✅ Hand over the IABS at first substantive contact if you haven't already — don't wait until you're standing in the driveway.
✅ Check lockbox access and confirm the showing time with the listing agent, especially for occupied properties — nothing worse than surprising a tenant.
✅ Look up the property beforehand — know the basics before the client asks.
Open Property Scout →
During the Showing
💡 Arrive early, turn on lights, open blinds — a bright house sells itself better than a dark one.
💡 Let the buyer walk and talk first — resist narrating every room immediately, let them react.
⚠️ Watch for safety basics: don't let a buyer wander into a garage or attic alone, and always know your own exit if something feels off. Never show a vacant property alone without telling someone your schedule.
💡 Take notes on what they react to — it's gold for narrowing down the next showings.
After the Showing
📋 Get feedback same-day while it's fresh.
Open Follow-Up Tracker →
📋 If they're not interested, ask why specifically — "too small" vs. "wrong layout" changes what you show next.
📋 Log everything — sloppy notes after showing #4 all blur together.
The Mistake Almost Every New Agent Makes

Showing too many houses in one day without a real strategy. 3–4 well-chosen showings beats 8 random ones. New agents often say yes to every listing a buyer points at instead of narrowing based on actual criteria first.
