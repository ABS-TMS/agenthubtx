The 5 Biggest Email Risks for Agents
1

Wire Fraud & Business Email Compromise

A hacker monitors your email, waits for a closing, then sends fake wire instructions that look like they came from you or your title company. Average loss: $50,000–$150,000.

Your E&O will NOT cover this
2

Unencrypted Text Messages

Sending contracts, financial statements, or client personal data via standard SMS is not secure. If intercepted, you're liable.

A cyber claim — not covered by standard E&O
3

Personal Gmail for Business Transactions

Free Gmail has no data processing agreement, no admin controls, and no guaranteed retention. If breached, Google bears no liability — you do.

4

No Two-Factor Authentication

One phishing email + no 2FA = complete account takeover in seconds. The hacker then has access to every client email, document, and contact in your account.

5

Client Data on Personal Devices

Contracts, driver's licenses, bank statements saved on your personal phone. Phone stolen or hacked = data breach.

Texas law requires you to notify every affected client
8 Things to Do Right Now — All Free
✓

Turn on 2-Factor Authentication

myaccount.google.com → Security → 2-Step Verification. Takes 3 minutes. Stops 99% of account takeovers.

✓

Check for hidden email forwarding rules

Gmail Settings → See all settings → Forwarding. Hackers silently forward your emails to monitor transactions. Check this today.

✓

Never click wire instructions from email alone

Always call the title company using a number you looked up independently — never from an email — before any wire transfer.

✓

Use Google Drive links instead of email attachments

Shared Drive links with access controls are safer than attachments which can be intercepted and modified in transit.

✓

Never use public Wi-Fi for transaction work

Coffee shops, hotels, open houses. Use your phone hotspot instead. Always.

✓

Set up login alerts

myaccount.google.com → Security → Recent security activity → turn on alerts for new device logins.

✓

Check what apps have access to your Gmail

myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps. Revoke anything you don't recognize or no longer use.

✓

Use unique passwords + 2FA everywhere — not just Gmail

Your CRM, MLS login, and transaction management tools hold the same sensitive client data as your inbox. Reused passwords are one of the most common ways these accounts get compromised. A password manager makes unique passwords painless — set it up once and stop reusing the same login everywhere.

The One Call That Prevents Wire Fraud
90 Seconds That Can Save $150,000

Before EVERY closing, call your title company using a phone number you looked up independently — NOT one from an email — and say:

"I'm calling to verbally confirm the wire instructions for [client name] closing on [date]. Can you read them to me?"
This one call has saved agents $50,000–$150,000. It takes 90 seconds.
