Advertising Rules Quick Reference

TREC Rules 535.154 & 535.155 — what's required, allowed, and prohibited

This is a practice aid for ABS agents, not a complete restatement of TREC Rules 535.154 and 535.155 or the Real Estate License Act. When in doubt on a specific ad, ask before you post. What you can say to a buyer or seller in marketing without a separate consult — cite the rule when someone asks why. Not a substitute for reading the actual rule on a close call.
Rule 535.155 — Broader Than Most Agents Assume
What Counts as "Advertising"
535.155

Any communication designed to attract the public to use real estate services — signs, flyers, texts, social media, email blasts, your website, business cards.

535.155

Does NOT include a communication to your own current client — that's not regulated as an ad.

535.155

A building sign, a yard sign, and a social media post are all treated the same way under this rule.

SB 2212 (2017) Changed This — Don't Assume the Old Rule
"Broker" or "Agent" Labels
Tex. Occ. Code

You are NOT required to label yourself "broker" or "agent" in an ad — that mandate was removed in 2017.

Tex. Occ. Code

TREC also cannot require you to reference TREC or your license number in an ad.

535.155

A sign that does say "broker" or "agent" is still permitted — just not mandatory.

Disclosure First, Always
Rebates & Referral Fees
535.155

A buyer rebate is allowed, but the ad must disclose it's subject to the seller's consent and any restrictions.

535.155

If you recommend a service provider (inspector, mover, lender) and may be paid for the referral, the ad must disclose that.

535.220(e)(3)

Inspectors specifically may not pay for referrals or list placement — know this if you're advertising alongside one.

Same Rules Apply — Character Limits Don't Exempt You
Social Media Specifically
TREC guidance

All the same disclosure rules apply on social media — there's no carve-out for Facebook, Instagram, etc.

TREC guidance

If a platform's character limit is a problem, a hyperlink labeled "TREC Disclosure" pointing to the required info is acceptable.

Rule 535.155(b) — The Most Commonly Missed Item
The Broker's Name Requirement
535.155(b)(1)

Every ad must include the broker's name in a readily noticeable location.

535.155(b)(1)

The broker's name must appear at least half the size of the largest contact info for any agent, associate, or team name in the ad.

535.155(b)(3)

A logo containing the broker's full name satisfies this, as long as the name itself (not the whole logo) meets the size rule.

Rule 535.154 — Register It Before You Use It
Team & Assumed Names
535.154(d)

Any assumed business name must be registered with TREC before it appears in an ad.

535.154(g)

A sales agent CANNOT use a name implying they run the brokerage (e.g. "Sally's Properties," "White Real Estate").

535.154(a)(5)

An agent CAN use their own name with "Team" or "Group" if the broker's name is also included and the team name is registered.

Only Claim What You Actually Did
"Just Sold" & Past Sale Claims
TREC FAQ

You may say you "sold" a property only if you personally worked with the buyer or seller in that transaction.

TREC FAQ

Sending a "Just Sold" postcard for a property you had no role in is misleading, even without using the word "sold" directly.

The Core Line Not to Cross
Misleading or Deceptive Advertising
§1101.652

TREC can suspend or revoke a license for advertising that is misleading or likely to deceive the public.

535.154

This includes materially false claims, advertising a listing without the listing broker's permission, and leaving outdated/closed listings up.
