The Seller's Obligations

  • Come from the contract itself
  • Deadlines: utilities, disclosures, survey, repairs, possession items
  • If missed: buyer remedies, termination rights, or post-closing liability
  • Tracked on the Seller Obligations Checklist

Your Obligations

  • Come from statute and your listing agreement
  • Fiduciary duties: care, obedience, loyalty, disclosure, confidentiality, accounting
  • If missed: license discipline, E&O exposure, DTPA / fraud claims naming the brokerage
  • Mapped to every deadline in this module
Where Your Duties Come From

Four legal sources establish what you owe a seller client — and one of them works very differently than it does on the buyer side.

TRELA §1101.557

A broker who represents a party owes that party the duties of a fiduciary.

Prop. Code §5.008

The disclosure duty is the seller's statutory burden — not the agent's. Your duty is narrower and explained next.

TRELA — Known Material Facts

You must disclose known structural defects and material facts — regardless of what the seller chooses to disclose.

TRELA §1101.558

You must disclose, on first contact, that you represent the seller.

Whose Disclosure Duty Is It?

The Seller Completes It

  • Texas Property Code §5.008 places the disclosure burden on the seller, not you
  • Courts have held agents are not liable for a seller's inaccurate disclosure unless the agent had reason to know it was false
  • Never fill out the Seller's Disclosure Notice for your client — doing so increases your liability and theirs

Your Duty Is Narrower

  • Disclose known structural defects and material facts you are personally aware of
  • Step in if you have reason to believe the seller's disclosure is false or incomplete
  • Confirm the notice was delivered on or before the effective date — a late delivery gives the buyer a 7-day walk right

Routing the Disclosures & Survey

Disclosure Duties

  • Confirm the Seller's Disclosure Notice goes out by the effective date — late delivery gives the buyer a 7-day walk right
  • Same urgency for the Water Disclosure if 7I(2) is checked
  • Never complete the form for your seller — review it, don't author it

Survey Duties

  • If 6C(1) is checked, confirm the existing survey and T-47 affidavit go to both buyer and title company
  • If 6C(3) is checked, confirm the seller has ordered and paid for a new survey
  • Calendar both deadlines the day the contract is signed — don't wait for title company reminders
WITHIN 20 DAYS Title Company Authorization
Authorize the title company to deliver the title commitment and exception documents to the buyer within 20 days of receiving the contract — a delay here stalls the buyer's entire objection timeline.
Para 6B

Confirm the title company received the contract and started the clock.

Para 6B

Follow up directly if the commitment hasn't gone out by day 15 — don't wait until day 20.

Care

A late commitment compresses the buyer's objection window and increases dispute risk for your seller.

Protecting Access and Property Rights
Care

Coordinate reasonable property access for the buyer and buyer's inspectors — balance the seller's privacy with the buyer's contractual right.

Care / Disclosure

Confirm any hydrostatic testing is authorized separately, in writing, before it happens.

Para 4

Advise the seller not to sign any new lease, amend an existing lease, or convey any interest in the property without the buyer's written consent.

CURE PERIOD — 15 DAYS Title & Survey Objections
Your seller has 15 days to cure timely buyer objections — but is not obligated to incur expense beyond reasonable effort (Para 6D).
Para 6D

Calendar the 15-day cure period the moment the buyer's written objection is received.

Loyalty

Help the seller distinguish "reasonable effort" from costly cures they are not contractually required to make.

Accounting

Document every step taken to cure — or the reasoning for declining — in case of dispute later.

Repairs, Removals & Restoration
Para 7F

Confirm all agreed repairs use licensed or commercially engaged contractors — not a handyman favor.

Para 7F

Confirm required permits were pulled for completed repairs.

Para 7F — Accounting

Collect documentation: scope of work and proof of payment for every repair, before closing.

Para 2D / 7F

Confirm excluded items (Para 2D) are removed and any transferable warranties are assigned to the buyer.

Casualty Damage & Mold History
If fire or casualty damages the property after the effective date, the seller must restore it before closing — flag this to your seller and their insurer immediately.
Para 14

Confirm restoration is complete and documented before the closing date.

Para 6E(11)

If the property was mold-remediated in the prior 5 years, confirm certificates are ready to hand the buyer.

Care

Missing remediation certificates at closing creates last-minute delays — request them early.

Getting the Seller to the Table Clean

Documents & Taxes

  • Confirm the general warranty deed has no exceptions beyond Para 6
  • Confirm tax statements/certificates show no delinquent taxes
  • Review all notices, releases, affidavits, and loan documents with the seller before signing

Fees & Withholding

  • Confirm private transfer fees are paid per the contract (Para 9B(5))
  • Confirm any residential service contract reimbursement to the buyer is calculated correctly (Para 7H)
  • Confirm a FIRPTA affidavit is ready — if the seller is a foreign person, the buyer must withhold from proceeds (Para 20A)
The Smart Home Handoff
A new compliance category under the 05-04-2026 form. Don't let this slip — it's an easy, avoidable post-closing dispute.
Para 10A

Confirm property is delivered in present or required condition, ordinary wear and tear excepted.

Para 10B(1)

Help the seller compile a written list of every access code, username, password, and app for smart devices.

Para 10B(2)

Confirm the seller has terminated and removed all personal device connections to smart devices before handoff.

The One-Line Test
It's Their Disclosure. Your Diligence.

The seller owns the disclosure obligation — but you own making sure every deadline is met, every document is routed on time, and every claim the seller makes has nothing in your file contradicting it.

  • Calendar every deadline the day the contract is signed — not the day before it's due.
  • Confirm escrow receipt, title company authorization, and survey delivery in writing.
  • Never complete the Seller's Disclosure Notice yourself — review it, don't author it.
  • Document repairs, certificates, and the smart-home handoff before closing day.
