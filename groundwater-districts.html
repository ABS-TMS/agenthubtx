If you or your broker are unsure how a district applies to a property, or don't understand the contract, addenda, or disclosure requirements involved, consult an attorney who understands these responsibilities.
Groundwater district boundaries don't follow county lines cleanly — the same district can cover multiple counties, and a single county can span more than one district. Always verify the specific district for a property yourself. This list is organized around the "SELLER'S DISCLOSURE ABOUT GROUNDWATER AND SURFACE WATER RIGHTS" form and Paragraph 1B of the contract, which defines a Groundwater District as: a local or regional Groundwater Conservation District, Underground Water Conservation District, Subsidence District, or other special district or authority that regulates the drilling or operation of water wells. Not all land in Texas is subject to a Groundwater District.
